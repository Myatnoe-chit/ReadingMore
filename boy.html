The Boy in the Striped Pajamas

Overview

*The Boy in the Striped Pajamas* is a novel by John Boyne, published in 2006. Set during World War II, it tells the story of a young boy named Bruno who befriends a boy on the other side of a concentration camp fence. The novel explores themes of innocence, friendship, and the horrors of the Holocaust.

About the Author

John Boyne

John Boyne is an Irish author who has written several novels for adults and children. He is best known for *The Boy in the Striped Pajamas*, which has been adapted into a feature film. His works often explore themes of history, identity, and moral dilemmas.

Key Themes

  • Innocence and Ignorance: The story is told from the perspective of Bruno, a young boy who does not fully understand the horrors of the concentration camp.
  • Friendship: The bond between Bruno and Shmuel, despite the barriers between them, highlights the innocence of childhood friendship.
  • Humanity vs. Inhumanity: The novel contrasts the innocence of the boys with the cruelty of the Holocaust.
